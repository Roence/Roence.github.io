Roence Youth Foundation

  • Vision
Roence Youth Foundation equips youths in Northern Uganda with resources and skills to savings groups and engaging in climate-smart economic activites, with a aim to make positive living attractive. As one body with the community, we uphold our commitment to diversity, inclusion & equity, and consider them essential elements of a vibrant environment and fundamental to our organization’s mission. As an initiative who is tasked with changing the odds for high-potential teens from within the communities, we are determined to foster an environment of inclusion, diversity, openness, and respect. We embrace and celebrate the differences including disability, immigration status, religion, gender, age, socioeconomic status, language, sexual orientation, and ethnicity. Roence Youth Foundation provides young people with opportunity, free from judgement and helps them envision a future worth investing in. And even more importantly, we encourage them to make a difference with the limited resources available and to pursue their dreams.

Healthy Food

Africa’s health depends on improved nutrition. Nutrition is a social and economic determinant of health that must be always be prioritized. Stakeholders need to be accountable for framing policies and implementing programmes that will effectively reduce preventable risks to health. Nutritional deficiencies are still major causes of death and disease in third-world countries.

Pure Water

The water crisis impacts about 4 out of every 10 people across the globe: 2.1 billion people lack access to safely managed drinking water sources, and 4.5 billion lack safely managed sanitation services. And even though sub-Saharan Africa only represents 12% of the global population, it contains 70% of the global burden of HIV and has the hightest number of young population. Nearly 50% of those still affected by the water crisis live in sub-Saharan Africa, with 325 million people still lacking access to clean water and 644 million lacking an improved sanitation facility. The UN states, “Water is our most precious resource – we must use it more responsibly. We must balance all of society’s water needs while ensuring the poorest youths don’t get left behind.” Training community members on protecting and maintaining their well or water point, so that they will continue to have access to clean water for years to come

Health Care

HIV/AIDS is among the top public health concerns and causes of mortality and morbidity in Africa, with 70% of all AIDS deaths currently occurring in the region. In 2011, there were an estimated 1.8 million new HIV infections in the region: the world’s highest. The marked emotional, sexual, and psychological transformations associated with adolescence increase experimentation with behaviors and practices that can have longstanding implications for their health and wellbeing. Unfortunately, many young people in Africa still lack comprehensive and correct HIV knowledge and their access to sexual and reproductive health services is often limited. Although young people’s sexual and reproductive health has received significant attention from practisoners, further evidence is needed on ways to address the sexual and reproductive health challenges that young people face. For example, existing research shows low levels of HIV testing among youth, particularly among males and family planning methods among young people. Youth in Africa face substantial barriers in accessing health services: age- and marital status-related barriers; lack of confidentiality; fear of mistreatment; inconvenient hours and locations of facilities; high costs of services; and limited knowledge of available services. With respect to marital status, the use of sexual and reproductive health services is often a challenge for unmarried young people in contexts where pre-marital sexual activity is proscribed.
